# Level 4 Pages Section Summary

These are the supporting layout and utility pages that power the site’s main content pages. They are not standalone marketing pages in the same way as Level 1–3 pages, but they still shape the overall user experience and message.

## 1. `header.html`

### Main section
- Navbar / top navigation
  - What it tries to tell: "Nuvue Studio is a professional digital marketing agency with clear main navigation and a strong brand presence."
- Services dropdown
  - What it tries to tell: "The agency offers SEO, social media marketing, content marketing, media production, branding design, and website development."
- Pages dropdown
  - What it tries to tell: "The site includes supporting pages such as case studies, testimonials, team, and FAQ."
- Contact CTA in nav
  - What it tries to tell: "Visitors should contact the business directly for services."
- Phone number area
  - What it tries to tell: "The agency is reachable and ready to be contacted immediately."

## 2. `footer.html`

### Main sections
- Brand intro block
  - What it tries to tell: "Nuvue Studio wants visitors to remember the agency’s value proposition: digital presence elevation."
- Quick links
  - What it tries to tell: "The site is structured so visitors can move to key actions like Home, About, Services, Contact, and Submissions."
- Services links
  - What it tries to tell: "The agency highlights its main service offerings again in the footer for easy access."
- Contact information
  - What it tries to tell: "The business provides direct contact details and location information for trust and credibility."
- Social media icons
  - What it tries to tell: "The company is active on major platforms and invites visitors to connect."
- Legal links
  - What it tries to tell: "The agency presents a professional business structure with terms and privacy support."

## 3. `sidebar.html`

### Main sections
- Mobile/off-canvas sidebar menu
  - What it tries to tell: "On smaller screens, the user still gets quick navigation and easy access to the main site sections."
- Main navigation links
  - What it tries to tell: "Visitors can reach Home, About, Services, Contact, and other essential pages from one compact menu."
- Dropdown menus for Services and Pages
  - What it tries to tell: "The site organizes its offering into clear categories for easier browsing."
- Close button / interaction controls
  - What it tries to tell: "The menu is user-friendly and can be dismissed quickly."

## 4. `search-form.html`

### Main sections
- Search overlay
  - What it tries to tell: "The site supports quick content discovery so users can search for specific information without navigating manually."
- Search input form
  - What it tries to tell: "The website is designed to help visitors find pages, posts, and information rapidly."
- Close button
  - What it tries to tell: "The search widget is lightweight and easy to dismiss when not needed."

## 5. `form_submission.html`

### Main sections
- Banner with breadcrumb
  - What it tries to tell: "This page is the admin/lead review view of all contact form submissions."
- Submission display heading
  - What it tries to tell: "The site collects and organizes inbound inquiries from visitors."
- Dynamic submissions list
  - What it tries to tell: "The business can review contact requests efficiently from the website interface."

## Overall Level 4 page intent

Level 4 pages are mostly structural and operational pages. They help the website by:
- improving navigation,
- supporting mobile menu flow,
- enabling search,
- showing contact data and social proof,
- and giving internal visibility into submitted inquiries.

These pages do not usually carry the main marketing story by themselves, but they are essential to how the website functions and how smoothly the visitor moves through the brand experience.
