# Level 0 Pages Section Summary

These are the foundational page-shell and utility pages that support the entire website experience. They are the lowest-level structural elements in the site architecture and usually provide navigation, search, footer, and lead collection behavior.

## 1. `header.html`

### Main section
- Navbar / top navigation
  - What it tries to tell: "Nuvue Studio is an established digital marketing agency with a clear and professional top-level site navigation."
- Services dropdown
  - What it tries to tell: "The business offers multiple digital services such as SEO, social media marketing, content marketing, media production, branding and design, and website development."
- Pages dropdown
  - What it tries to tell: "The website also contains supporting pages like case studies, testimonials, team, and FAQ."
- Contact CTA
  - What it tries to tell: "Visitors should not hesitate to contact the agency directly."
- Contact phone area
  - What it tries to tell: "The business is reachable and ready for immediate communication."

## 2. `footer.html`

### Main sections
- Brand positioning block
  - What it tries to tell: "Nuvue Studio wants visitors to remember the agency’s central value promise: helping businesses elevate their digital presence."
- Quick links
  - What it tries to tell: "The site wants users to quickly access important pages like Home, About, Services, and Contact."
- Services list
  - What it tries to tell: "The agency reinforces its full-service offer through the footer area."
- Contact information
  - What it tries to tell: "The business provides trust signals through visible phone, email, and address details."
- Social media links
  - What it tries to tell: "The brand maintains active external channels and encourages users to connect socially."
- Legal links
  - What it tries to tell: "The website appears professional and gives visitors a standard trust framework."

## 3. `sidebar.html`

### Main sections
- Mobile/off-canvas navigation menu
  - What it tries to tell: "On smaller screens, visitors can still browse the site quickly and clearly."
- Services submenu
  - What it tries to tell: "The site organizes its services into a compact, usable navigation pattern."
- Pages submenu
  - What it tries to tell: "The user can access supporting pages such as pricing, case studies, testimonials, team, and FAQ."
- Close action and dismissal behavior
  - What it tries to tell: "The sidebar is designed for convenience and a smoother mobile user experience."

## 4. `search-form.html`

### Main sections
- Search overlay
  - What it tries to tell: "The site supports easy and fast content discovery without forcing visitors to browse manually."
- Search input form
  - What it tries to tell: "The site is organized so that useful content can be found quickly."
- Close button
  - What it tries to tell: "The search experience is lightweight and easy to exit."

## 5. `form_submission.html`

### Main sections
- Page banner with breadcrumb
  - What it tries to tell: "This page acts as the lead review / submission panel for contact form entries."
- Submission listing area
  - What it tries to tell: "The business can inspect and track incoming inquiries in one place."
- Dynamic data display
  - What it tries to tell: "The website can keep a visible record of incoming leads for internal follow-up."

## Overall Level 0 page intent

Level 0 pages are the structural foundation of the website. They are not usually built to sell one service directly, but they are crucial because they:
- provide navigation,
- reinforce brand presence,
- support mobile usability,
- enable search,
- and help the business manage leads and contact inquiries.

In short, Level 0 pages shape how the whole site is used and how users move through the brand experience.
